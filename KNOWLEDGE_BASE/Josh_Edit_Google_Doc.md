===========================
VIDEO TRIBUTE FRAMEWORK IDEAS (ARCHIVE)
===========================

IDEA 1: THE CHRONOLOGICAL JOURNEY
Flow: Starts with childhood (growing up with Ben and Natalie). Moves into high school and military service. Climaxes with meeting and marrying Linsey, and big family gatherings.
Song Ideas: 
- "In My Life" (The Beatles) for the early years.
- "You Should Be Here" (Cole Swindell) for the military years.
- "Good Riddance" (Green Day) for the finale.

IDEA 2: THE "SAVE A PLACE FOR ME" THEME
Flow: Tied to the 'Save a Place For Me' photo. Opens with the bond between Josh and his brother Ben. Transitions into his military service and marriage, and ends on a peaceful note signifying he is reunited with Ben, Aunt Cindy, Cousin Kyle, and Bill.
Song Ideas: 
- "Save a Place for Me" (Matthew West)
- "See You Again" (Wiz Khalifa)

IDEA 3: THE CORE PILLARS (Thematic Approach)
Flow: Instead of chronological, the video is split into 'Chapters'. 
- Chapter 1: The Family Man (Linsey, Parents, Grandparents). 
- Chapter 2: Brothers in Arms (The military buddies). 
- Chapter 3: The Fun-Loving Guy (High school, friends, the mud-on-face photos).
Song Ideas: 
- "Brother" (NEEDTOBREATHE)
- "Photograph" (Ed Sheeran)

IDEA 4: REVERSE CHRONOLOGICAL (The Legacy)
Flow: Starts with the man he became (recent photos with Linsey). Slowly transitions back through time, through the military days, high school, and ends on childhood photos with his parents, showing the complete circle of his life.
Song Ideas: 
- "Landslide" (Fleetwood Mac)
- "Tears in Heaven" (Eric Clapton)

IDEA 5: DOCU-STYLE NARRATIVE (Cinematic)
Flow: Using the Face ID folders to do quick-cut montages of specific people while someone (like Jeff or Linsey) speaks a voiceover. When military is mentioned, military photos flash. 
Song Ideas: 
- Cinematic instrumental scoring building into a powerful acoustic track.
